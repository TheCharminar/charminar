Materials and Construction

The use of material is also an important feature in the process of the Charminar's construction. The choice of durable materials, namely granite as a load-bearing body, limestone blocks as wall material, and lime mortar as a binding agent made the construction weather resistant against rains and hot temperatures. Thus, granite comprises the load-bearing body of the monument and limestone makes up the walls and decorations. However, the durability of the lime mortar is the key element since it gets harder upon meeting with air, and the overall construction is strengthened over time.

Moreover, from a technical standpoint, the monument's construction demonstrates a high level of skillful engineering design that is typical for the buildings of the 16th century. It is known that the building process was intended to create the perfect symmetry of four sides which allows distributing the load evenly. Moreover, arches allow transferring loads in an efficient way, and mminarets' design impliese that they have solid foundations and staircases inside which increases their strength. Furthmore, stone ornaments can also be regrarded as a significant feature of the Charminar construction.
